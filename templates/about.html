About This Project

AI-Powered Lumpy Skin Disease Detection — PengwinTech Solutions · February 2026

Project Objective

This project develops a web-based computer vision application that analyses cattle images and identifies visible signs of Lumpy Skin Disease (LSD) using a trained YOLOv8 object detection model. It is built for educational and research purposes only as a final-year Computer Science engineering project.

The platform bridges the gap between cutting-edge AI research and real-world livestock healthcare — making early disease screening accessible to farmers and field staff who may not have immediate veterinary support.

What is Lumpy Skin Disease?

Lumpy Skin Disease is a viral disease caused by the Lumpy Skin Disease Virus (LSDV), a member of the Capripoxvirus genus. It primarily affects cattle and water buffalo, causing characteristic skin nodules (2–5 cm), fever, reduced milk production, and in severe cases, death.

AI & Computer Vision Approach

Computer Vision is the field of AI that enables machines to interpret and understand visual information from the real world. In this project, we apply it to automated cattle health screening — treating visible LSD nodules as objects to be detected in images.

The detection pipeline follows these stages:

1
Image Preprocessing

OpenCV resizes, normalises, and prepares the uploaded image to the model's required 640×640 input resolution.

2
Feature Extraction

YOLOv8's CSPDarknet backbone extracts multi-scale feature maps capturing texture, shape, and spatial patterns of lesions.

3
Detection & Classification

The detection head produces bounding boxes with class scores. Non-Maximum Suppression filters overlapping predictions.

4
Result Generation

Lesion count, maximum confidence score, and severity grade are computed and stored alongside the annotated image.

About YOLOv8

YOLOv8 (You Only Look Once, version 8) is a state-of-the-art real-time object detection framework developed by Ultralytics. It is currently one of the fastest and most accurate detection architectures available, making it ideal for field-deployed medical screening tools.
